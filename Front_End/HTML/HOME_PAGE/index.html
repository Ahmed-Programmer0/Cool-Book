The Story of the Boy Who Cried Wolf

It is said that in a small village lived a shepherd who tended his sheep every day, taking them to the beautiful green mountains to graze and eat the grass. In the evening, he would return home to rest. One day, while the shepherd was with his sheep, he felt very bored. He had an idea to play a prank on the villagers, so he went to them asking for help and began shouting, "Help me! Help me! The wolf is coming! He wants to eat my sheep!" The villagers rushed to his aid and to save the sheep from the wolf. When they arrived, they found nothing and discovered that the shepherd had been playing a trick on them. Annoyed by his joke and lies, they returned to their work. He repeated this trick several times. Then one day, while the shepherd was in the mountains as usual, a large wolf appeared and attacked the sheep, eating them one by one. The shepherd was terrified and rushed to the village, running and shouting for help. But the villagers ignored his cries, knowing he had lied before. No one came to his aid. The shepherd returned to his sheep, only to find they were all gone; the wolf had eaten them. When the villagers learned what had happened, they told him that his previous lies had prevented them from believing him this time when he truly needed help. Thus, the shepherd lost his job and the villagers' trust. He learned a harsh lesson: lying has bad consequences, and anyone who lies once, even jokingly, will never be believed again.

The Story of the Strange Man

At the far end of the neighborhood lived a small house where a woman named Amal lived with her three children. Every morning she would leave for work, leaving her children alone at home. In the evening, she would return with food, stand at the door, and begin to sing: "Oh little bird, Mama has lots of food!" so that the children would recognize their mother by her voice and open the door for her. This happened every day. One day, their mother was late returning home, and the children were waiting for her. Suddenly, there was a knock at the door. The eldest son called out, "Who's there?" No one answered. After a few minutes, they heard someone singing behind the door. The youngest son said happily, "It's Mama! She's back with food!" The eldest son replied, "No, that's not Mama's voice. Don't open the door." The youngest brother ignored his brother and opened the door. A strange man stood there. The children cried out, "Who is this?" The stranger attacked them and tried to steal from them. They began to scream, cry, and call for help. But no one heard them. Suddenly, the mother arrived, and the stranger, frightened, ran away. The children gathered around their mother, and she said, "Didn't I warn you against opening the door to strangers?" The children apologized to their mother and promised not to do it again.

The Story of Luqman the Wise

Luqman gained much knowledge and wisdom at the hands of our master David, peace be upon him, until he gained the love of God Almighty, who bestowed upon him a great abundance of knowledge and wisdom, which qualified him to be a wise man and Gods successor on earth. He became known as Luqman the Wise, and the reason for this is due to his great shrewdness and skill in resolving disputes, as he worked as a judge for the Children of Israel. One of the stories that demonstrates his great wisdom and intelligence is that one day his master brought him a sheep and said, "Slaughter this sheep and bring me the two best morsels from it." So he slaughtered it and brought him both the tongue and the heart. Then the next day his master said to him, "Slaughter this sheep and bring me the two worst morsels from it." So God brought him both the heart and the tongue. His master was amazed by this, and Luqman the Wise said, "There is nothing better than these two when they are good, and nothing worse than them when they are bad." At that point, his master ordered him to be freed because of his wisdom and intelligence. After his freedom, he decided to leave Egypt and travel to Palestine. This was during the time of Prophet David, peace be upon him, where he worked as a servant for him. Prophet David noticed this man's wisdom and appointed him as a judge over the Children of Israel. Later, he became the Chief Justice.

The Story of the Bedouin

There lived during the time of the Prophet, may God bless him and grant him peace, a Bedouin who believed in God and His Prophet. One day he came to the Messenger of God asking him to allow him to fight in one of the battles. The Bedouin hoped for martyrdom in the cause of God, and the Messenger of God allowed him to participate in one of them. Indeed, he participated with the Prophet Muhammad, may God bless him and grant him peace, and his companions in that battle, but he did not attain martyrdom. After the battle ended and victory was achieved for the Muslims, the spoils were brought to be divided, and each one received his share. But the Bedouin refused to take it and went to the Prophet Muhammad, may God bless him and grant him peace, and told him that he did not participate in the battle and fight in it out of greed or desire for spoils, but rather he did it for the sake of God and out of desire to win martyrdom and Paradise, which awaits those who are killed in the cause of God. The noble Messenger replied to him: If you are truthful to God, He will be truthful to you, meaning that God would grant this Bedouin his wish if he was truthful in his desire and intention before anything else. And this is what the Bedouin did. He was sincere in his devotion to God with all his being. When another battle took place, he hastened to participate, his sincere intention being to be martyred in the cause of God. He was martyred during the fighting. When the battle ended, the Companions of the Messenger of God brought him to their companion. Upon seeing him, the Prophet asked, "Is this him?" The Companions answered in the affirmative. The Prophet, peace and blessings be upon him, said, "He was truthful to God, so God was truthful to him." The Messenger of God then performed the ritual washing and shrouding of the Bedouin, and prayed for him, saying, "O God, this is Your servant who went forth as a migrant in Your cause and was killed as a martyr. I bear witness to this."

The Story of the Prophet and His Honesty with the Arab Tribes

A dispute arose one day among the Arab tribes living around the Kaaba. The cause of this dispute was each tribe's desire to have the honor of placing the Black Stone in its place in the Kaaba after its reconstruction. The dispute escalated and continued for some time until Umayya ibn al-Mughirah al-Makhzumi suggested that they decide the matter of their disagreement by letting the first person to enter the gate of the Sacred Mosque decide. The tribes agreed and waited for the person who would enter. Then, the Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him) entered. Upon seeing him, they exclaimed, "This is the trustworthy one!" The Prophet Muhammad was known for his truthfulness and trustworthiness. The tribes then submitted their decision regarding the Black Stone to him. When they had finished, he asked for a cloth to be brought. When it was brought, he placed the Black Stone in it and asked each tribe to hold one end of the cloth and lift it together. They did as he asked and carried it to its place. There, the noble Prophet took it and placed it in its position, and the dispute among the tribes ended. All of this was thanks to the trustworthiness and truthfulness that characterized the Prophet Muhammad (peace and blessings be upon him). And peace be upon him.
